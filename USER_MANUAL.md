# Visitor Management System — User Manual

---

## Welcome

This manual explains how to use the Visitor Management System (VMS) — the tool your organisation uses to register visitors, print their passes, and keep track of who is on the premises at any time.

There is nothing complicated to install or set up. VMS runs in a web browser on the computer at your reception desk, and on your own phone or laptop if you are a host.

---

## Who Uses VMS and What They See

VMS has two types of users. Your administrator will have set up your account as one of these when they created your login.

| Your Role | What you do in VMS |
|---|---|
| **Security / Guard** | Register visitors when they arrive, print their passes, mark them as checked out when they leave, search visit history |
| **Host** | Receive a notification the moment your visitor arrives at reception, see a live list of your current visitors |

When you log in, VMS automatically takes you to the right screen for your role. You will never see buttons or pages that are not meant for you.

---

## Part 1 — Signing In

**Step 1.** Open the VMS link in your web browser. You will see the sign-in screen.

**Step 2.** Type your **phone number** and **password** in the boxes provided.

**Step 3.** Click **Sign in**.

- If you are security staff, you will land on the **Check In** screen ready to register a visitor.
- If you are a host, you will land on **My Visitors**.

> **Forgot your password?** Contact your administrator. Passwords cannot be reset from within VMS.

> **Wrong phone number or password?** A red message will appear under the form. Check for typos and try again.

---

## Part 2 — For Security / Guard Staff

As a guard or receptionist, you have three tools in the top navigation bar:

- **Check In** — register a new visitor
- **Active** — see everyone currently on the premises
- **History** — search past visits

---

### 2.1 Checking In a Visitor

This is what you do every time someone walks through the door to meet a colleague.

**Step 1.** Click **Check In** in the top bar.

**Step 2.** Fill in the form:

| Field | What to enter | Required? |
|---|---|---|
| Visitor Name | The full name of the person arriving | Yes |
| Phone | Their mobile number | Optional |
| Host | The employee they are here to meet — pick from the dropdown list | Yes |
| Purpose of Visit | A short note, e.g. "Interview", "Delivery", "Meeting" | Optional |

**Step 3.** Take a photo (recommended).
- Click **Open Camera**. Your webcam will turn on.
- Ask the visitor to look at the camera, then click **Take Photo**.
- A small preview of the photo will appear.
- If the photo did not come out well, click **Retake** and try again.
- If you do not have a camera or do not wish to take a photo, simply skip this step.

**Step 4.** Click **Check In**.

What happens next:
- A green confirmation message appears: *"[Visitor Name] checked in — Pass #VMS-XXXXXX"*
- A **Visitor Pass** automatically opens in a new tab, ready to print.
- The host is notified on their phone or computer that their visitor has arrived.

---

### 2.2 Printing the Visitor Pass

After a successful check-in, a Visitor Pass page opens automatically. The pass shows:

- Property name and **VISITOR PASS** heading
- Visitor's photo (if taken)
- Visitor's name and unique pass number (e.g. VMS-AB12CD)
- Host's name
- Purpose of visit
- Check-in time

**To print:**
- Click the **Print** button on the page.
- Your browser's print dialog will open. Select your printer and print.
- Hand the printed pass to the visitor to wear while on premises.

**If the pass did not open automatically:**
- Check if your browser blocked a pop-up. Look for a notification at the top of the browser bar and allow pop-ups for this site.

**To close the pass tab without printing:**
- Click the **Close** button, or simply close the browser tab.

---

### 2.3 Viewing Active Visitors

Click **Active** in the top bar to see a live table of every visitor currently on the premises.

The table shows:
- Visitor photo and name
- Their phone number
- Which host they are visiting
- Purpose of visit
- Time they checked in

The green badge at the top right shows the total count of visitors currently inside.

**This screen updates automatically.** If a new visitor is checked in at reception, they appear here within seconds — you do not need to refresh the page.

---

### 2.4 Checking Out a Visitor

When a visitor leaves, you record their departure so the record is complete.

**Step 1.** Click **Active** in the top bar.

**Step 2.** Find the visitor's row in the table.

**Step 3.** Click the red **Check out** button on the right side of their row.

The visitor disappears from the Active list immediately. Their visit is now recorded as complete in History with the departure time.

---

### 2.5 Visit History

Click **History** in the top bar to see a full log of all past and present visits.

**Searching by name:**
- Type any part of a visitor's name or their host's name in the search box. The list filters as you type.

**Filtering by date:**
- Click the first date box and choose a start date.
- Click the second date box and choose an end date.
- The table will show only visits that happened between those two dates.
- Click **Clear** to remove the date filter.

**Reading the table:**

| Column | What it means |
|---|---|
| Visitor | Name and phone number |
| Host | The employee they visited |
| Purpose | Reason for the visit |
| Check In | Date and time they arrived |
| Check Out | Date and time they left |
| Duration | How long they were on premises. Shows **Active** in green if they are still here |

**Pagination:** If there are more than 20 records, use the **Previous** and **Next** buttons at the bottom to move between pages. The current range is shown, for example *"1–20 of 47"*.

## Part 3 — For Hosts

As a host, VMS keeps you informed the moment your visitor arrives — without you needing to watch a screen or wait for a call from reception.

---

### 3.1 Allowing Notifications on Your Device

The first time you sign into VMS on a device, your browser will ask whether you want to allow notifications from this site.

**Click Allow.**

This is how VMS sends you an instant alert when your visitor arrives. If you accidentally clicked Block, you will need to update your browser's notification settings for the VMS website. Ask your administrator if you need help with this.

> **Important:** Notifications work on the device and browser you used when you first logged in. If you use a different device or a different browser (e.g. Chrome vs Safari), sign in there too so that device is also registered.

---

### 3.2 Receiving an Arrival Notification

When the guard checks in a visitor who is coming to see you, you will receive a browser notification that looks like this:

> **Visitor Arrived**
> *John Smith has arrived at Acme HQ.*

- You will see this notification even if the VMS tab is not open in front of you.
- Clicking the notification takes you directly to your **My Visitors** page.

---

### 3.3 Your My Visitors Dashboard

Click **My Visitors** in the top bar at any time to see your current visitors.

**New arrival alerts (blue banner):**

When a visitor arrives, a blue highlighted alert appears at the top of the page:

> ● **John Smith has arrived**
> *Interview*

The pulsing blue dot means this is a new, unacknowledged arrival. Once you have noted it, click **Dismiss** to remove the banner. Dismissing the alert does not affect the visitor's record — it simply clears the notification from your screen.

**Active visitors table:**

Below the alerts, the table shows all visitors who are currently with you or waiting for you:

| Column | What it means |
|---|---|
| Visitor | Photo (or initials), name, and phone number |
| Purpose | Reason for their visit |
| Arrived | How long ago they checked in (e.g. "12m ago", "1h ago") |
| Badge | Their unique pass number |

**This screen updates live.** You do not need to refresh — new arrivals appear automatically.

---

### 3.4 When a Visitor Leaves

You do not need to do anything when a visitor leaves. The security guard at reception handles the check-out. Once they click Check Out, the visitor will disappear from your My Visitors list.

---

## Part 4 — Signing Out

When you are finished using VMS, always sign out — especially on a shared computer at reception.

**Step 1.** Look at the top-right corner of any page. You will see your name and role.

**Step 2.** Click **Sign out**.

You will be returned to the login screen. Your session is now closed.

> **Tip for hosts:** Signing out also unregisters your device from push notifications. If you are signing out on a device you use daily, remember to sign back in so you continue receiving arrival alerts.

## Part 5 — Quick Reference

### Security Staff — Daily Workflow

```
Visitor walks in
        ↓
Click "Check In" → fill the form → take photo → click "Check In"
        ↓
Visitor Pass opens → Print it → give to visitor
        ↓
Visitor leaves → Click "Active" → find visitor → click "Check out"
```

### Host — Daily Workflow

```
Notification pops up: "Your visitor has arrived"
        ↓
Click the notification → My Visitors page opens
        ↓
Read the arrival alert → click "Dismiss" when noted
        ↓
Visitor leaves (guard checks them out) → they disappear from your list
```

## Part 6 — Common Questions

**Q: The host dropdown is empty when I try to check in a visitor.**
A: No host accounts have been set up yet, or none belong to this property. Contact your administrator to add host accounts.

**Q: I checked in a visitor but the pass did not open.**
A: Your browser may be blocking pop-ups. Look for a pop-up blocked icon in the address bar, click it, and select "Always allow pop-ups from this site." Then check in the next visitor and the pass will open normally.

**Q: I am a host but I am not getting notifications.**
A: Make sure you clicked **Allow** when the browser asked for notification permission. Also check that your device's notification settings have not muted the browser. On some phones, go to Settings → Notifications → [Browser name] and make sure notifications are turned on.

**Q: A visitor was checked in by mistake. Can it be undone?**
A: Check out the visitor immediately using the **Active** screen. This closes their record. Contact your administrator if the record needs to be corrected in the history.

**Q: Can I see visits from a previous month?**
A: Yes. Click **History**, then use the date range filters to select any past period. There is no limit on how far back you can search.

**Q: What if two visitors arrive for the same host at the same time?**
A: Check them in one after the other — each is a separate entry. The host will receive a separate notification for each visitor, and both will appear on the host's My Visitors page.

**Q: My name or role shown in the top corner is wrong.**
A: Contact your administrator. Only administrators can change account details.

---

## Part 7 — Tips for a Smooth Experience

- **Keep the VMS tab open** at reception at all times. The Active Visitors screen is your live record of who is on the premises.
- **Always check out visitors** before the end of each shift. Any visitor still shown as "Active" at end of day should be investigated.
- **Use the Purpose field** consistently — "Delivery", "Interview", "Client Meeting", etc. — so History searches are meaningful.
- **Take the photo** whenever possible. It makes the visitor pass more professional and helps identify visitors if needed later.
- **Hosts: keep your browser signed in** on your work device. If you sign out and forget to sign back in, you will miss arrival notifications.
